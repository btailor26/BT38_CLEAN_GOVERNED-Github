Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started

This is a multi-channel inventory management system that helps you manage stock across multiple selling platforms like Amazon and eBay. It keeps all your inventory synchronized automatically, so when you sell on one platform, stock updates everywhere.

Go to the Stores page and click "Add Store". Choose your platform (Amazon or eBay), enter your store details, and follow the authentication process. Once connected, your listings will be imported automatically.
Stock Management

Always update stock in the Warehouse page. This is your single source of truth. When you change a quantity there, it automatically pushes to all connected marketplaces (Amazon, eBay, etc.).

The system works in two directions: 1) When you update warehouse stock, it pushes to all marketplaces. 2) When someone buys on a marketplace, that sale syncs back to update your warehouse stock. This keeps everything balanced automatically.

Product Groups are for managing variations of the same product (like different sizes or colors). When items are grouped, they share the same stock level. Update one, and all items in the group update together. Use groups when you have the same physical product listed with different SKUs.
Linking Products

Products are linked automatically by SKU. If you have "GAME-001" on Amazon and "GAME-001" on eBay, they will automatically connect to the same warehouse stock. When you update stock for GAME-001 in the warehouse, both Amazon and eBay listings update.

Use Product Groups! Create a group, add both SKUs to it, and they will share the same stock level. For example, if Amazon uses "AMZ-GAME-001" and eBay uses "EBAY-GAME-001", group them together and they'll stay synchronized.

The Admin Product Linking page (found under Settings) lets you view and manage how warehouse products connect to marketplace listings. You can: 1) See which SKUs are linked to Amazon/eBay listings 2) View blocked reasons when syncing fails 3) Link new products to marketplace IDs 4) Run bulk diagnostics and repairs 5) Ungroup products that were incorrectly grouped 6) Fix wrong mappings with the "Not Same Product" tool.

A listing becomes "Blocked" when the system cannot push stock updates to the marketplace. Common reasons: 1) Zero price - set a valid price 2) Warehouse stock not linked - link the product 3) Store disconnected - reconnect the store 4) Too many failures - use the Repair button to reset. To see the exact reason, go to Admin Product Linking and look at the red badge or click "View Blocked Reasons".

Select products using the checkboxes, then choose an action: 1) Run Diagnostics - checks for issues like blocked listings, missing links 2) Repair Links - attempts to fix blocked listings automatically 3) Push Selected - forces a stock update to marketplaces. These run as background jobs so you can continue working. Check the Jobs tab to see progress and any errors.

This is useful for bulk operations on hundreds of products at once. The count includes any filters you've applied (like platform or search), so you're only selecting products that match your current view.
Purchase Orders & Suppliers

There are two ways: 1) Quick method: Go to Warehouse, find the SKU, and update the quantity directly. 2) Proper method: Create a Purchase Order from a Supplier, receive the goods, and the system will update warehouse stock and track costs automatically.
Troubleshooting

Check these things: 1) Is the store connected and showing green status? 2) Is the listing linked to warehouse stock? (Check the Listings page) 3) Try clicking "Push" on the warehouse item to force a sync. If problems persist, check the Stores page for any authentication errors.

Amazon FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) and FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant) are treated as completely separate integrations. FBA: Amazon controls inventory at their warehouses - we can only import/read stock levels, cannot push changes. FBM: You control inventory - the warehouse is authoritative and pushes stock updates to Amazon. They use separate store connections, credentials, and sync rules. This prevents confusion and ensures accurate inventory management.

Amazon FBA inventory is controlled by Amazon at their fulfillment centers - you cannot directly change stock levels. The system imports FBA quantities for visibility but blocks push attempts because Amazon manages that inventory. To update FBA stock, you must send shipments to Amazon's warehouses through Seller Central. For merchant-fulfilled items (FBM), you can push from your warehouse.
Multi-Channel Fulfillment (MCF)

MCF lets you use your Amazon FBA inventory to fulfill orders from other channels (eBay, Etsy, your website). When an order comes from eBay, the system can automatically send it to Amazon for fulfillment using your FBA stock. Amazon picks, packs, and ships the order directly to your customer. This saves you from managing separate warehouse operations.

MCF uses your existing FBA inventory - the same stock you have at Amazon's warehouses. When an MCF order is created, Amazon deducts the stock from your FBA inventory and ships it. Important: MCF does NOT use your warehouse stock. It only works with products you already have stored at Amazon's fulfillment centers.

MCF offers three shipping speeds: 1) Standard (3-5 business days) - most economical option 2) Expedited (2-3 business days) - faster delivery 3) Priority (1-2 business days) - fastest option. Prices vary by speed and destination. The system calculates fees automatically before you confirm an MCF order.
Profit & Margin Tracking

The system calculates profit by subtracting all costs from the sale price: 1) Product cost (what you paid for the item) 2) Landed cost (import duties, shipping from supplier) 3) Platform fees (eBay final value fee, Amazon referral fee) 4) Fulfillment fees (FBA fees, MCF fees, or shipping labels) 5) VAT on fees. The result is your gross profit, shown as a percentage margin.

The system uses a 15% target margin as a baseline for profitability. When a sale would result in less than 15% margin, you'll see a warning. You can customize this target per product or per channel. Margin alerts help you avoid selling at a loss and identify products that need price adjustments.
